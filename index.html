About

I am an audio engineer based out of beautiful Portland, OR after a somewhat recent move from Brooklyn, NY and, before that, Austin, TX (“Live Music Capital of the World”). I’ve been doing sound since high school when I started recording my own music and running the console for my school’s theater and ensemble performances. It didn’t take long before I realized that I enjoyed mixing more than playing guitar and that I wanted to be an engineer professionally.

More than ten years later and I have been fortunate enough to gain an abundance of classroom and real world knowledge in the field of audio engineering. I have learned from the best and have worked with the best, all the while creating a philosophy built on positive collaborations with artists and a devotion to quality sound. I genuinely believe that an audience deserves the best whether the performance is for 5 or 5,000 people.

My goal in moving to Portland is to find a home that facilitates growth. My ideal environment is one that appreciates quality sound, where musicians are peers rather than nuisances, and professionalism is the norm. Perpetually interested in personal progress, I want to bring my skills and enthusiasm to a business that encourages my endless education; a home that I can grow with rather than out of.
